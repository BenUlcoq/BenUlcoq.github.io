Coding Bootcamps, Meetups and .. Twitter?

Published 21st September

When I sat down in the Coder Academy classroom on Monday morning I wasn’t sure what to expect. All I knew as that I was ready to jump into the world of programming, Ruby, loops and everything in between…

“You need to get Twitter!”

At first I thought maybe I’d mistakenly ended up in the wrong classroom. It wouldn’t have been too far-fetched; I’d only arrived in Brisbane 4 days prior after moving from the (relatively) sleepy Central Queensland town of Rockhampton. The early start had me feeling a little groggy.

After coming to my senses a bit more, I found out I was in fact in the right place. What I discovered was that my outlook for the course (and beyond!) was naive. Apparently there’s so much more to coding than sitting in a dark corner with headphones on plugging away at a keyboard endlessly!

Okay, maybe I’m exaggerating my naivety a little bit.

But I certainly wasn’t expecting there to be meetups and networking events nearly every single day (with PIZZA AND BEER). I had the fantastic experience of attending a Sectalks event last night. I tagged along with fellow ‘developer in the making’ Santiago (Check out his great posts here!). Although most of the presentation flew way over my head it was extremely interesting getting a sneak-peek into some of the things happening in and around Brisbane. There’s a bunch of awesome people doing some pretty amazing things every day and I can’t wait to immerse myself even further into the world of Brisbane tech.

All of this and I’ve barely even touched on the course itself! But hey, there’s sure to be plenty of that to come.

If you’re interested in getting some insight into a coding bootcamp at Coder Academy and life in Brisbane in general then keep an eye out — I’ll be posting here about my journey throughout the course.

Follow me on Twitter @BenUlcoq (It’s bare at the moment — but I’ll get there!)
